## Imported Claude Cowork project instructions

Context
The goals of this project are to understand what “frontier firms” look like in the age of AI and translate those patterns into a practical benchmark for Microsoft. This is sort of a revamp of the Tech Intensity study and follow-on projects we did from 2020-2022, but adapted to modern organizations and with a focus on operating models and the frontier. Our current research framework (WIP) has three main components:
Infrastructure: the technology foundation that powers AI-enabled work, including compute, cloud, models, data platforms, and the core technical systems required to train, serve, and scale AI.
Harness / architecture: the connective layer that makes AI usable in work, including how context is assembled, tools are called, agents are orchestrated, workflows are executed, outputs are validated, and guardrails are applied.
Operating capabilities: the organizational capabilities needed to convert AI into business value, including workflow redesign, governance, measurement, talent, roles, decision rights, behavior change, and emerging token / AI consumption economics.
We're applying this framework to develop the scorecard and benchmark in three phases:
Frontier research and framework development
In the first phase, we're conducting interviews with frontier startups and experts to understand what leading operating models look like in practice and what the steps are to get there. This will give us the high-maturity anchor for the benchmark and the maturity phases along the way to get there. We have 13 completed so far and are aiming for 25, but this is adapting as we determine how our time is best spent—there's no hard requirement from MSFT on the n for this phase.
Broader enterprise benchmarking
We will then use the framework to assess a larger set of companies across sectors. This phase is intended to show where enterprises stand today, how maturity varies by industry and function, and which practices appear most connected to business value. We plan to benchmark 200 companies (current plan is 1 human-led interview and 2 AI-moderated interviews per company) and conduct econometric analyses to understand which AI operating model practices are most strongly associated with business outcomes.
Quarterly updates
We will then switch to a quarterly-update cadence, benchmarking a set of companies and adapting the benchmark as necessary (i.e., as the frontier changes).
