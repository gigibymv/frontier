# Operating Capabilities — Slide Bullets

---

## 1. Org Structure & Composition

**Existing**
- Smaller team ("1 engineer builds")
- Middle-management layer reduced as coordination shifts up
- Older functions merging and roles being redesigned
- Focus on "no silos"
- Creation of AI steering committees (risk, strategy, and/or cost)
- AI champions who push adoption

**New from interviews**
- Non-engineers committing production code *(n=3)*
- Senior leaders expected to personally ship with AI *(n=2)*
- Dedicated platform team owns deployment; product teams consume it *(n=1–3)*
- "Context owner" — domain expert who maintains the AI knowledge layer *(n=2)*
- Automation shifts roles, doesn't eliminate them *(n=4)*

---

## 2. Talent Readiness

**Existing**
- AI fluency requirement for candidates and recruiters
- Interviews with live build of AI or plan.md
- Curriculums per function to increase AI literacy for reskilling
- Classifying skills and roles by replaceability
- Novel roles focused on orchestration and governance

**New from interviews**
- Orchestration replaces coding as core engineering skill *(n=2–3)*
- Hallucination detection as an explicit hiring criterion *(n=1–2)*
- Non-engineers producing production-grade output *(n=3)*
- Hiring decisions include "will this role exist in 2 years?" *(n=1)*
- Domain knowledge deprioritized — AI knowledge layer substitutes for it *(n=1)*
- Personal AI subscription used as a signal of genuine fluency *(n=1)*

---

## 3. Performance Management

**Existing**
- AI leverage as explicit review dimension
- Promoted on AI leverage AND on helping others scale
- Token consumption secondary to orchestration efficiency

**New from interviews**
- AI proficiency as a binary gate, not a graded criterion *(n=2)*
- Orchestration quality vs. volume of AI usage — gap no one has solved *(n=0 implemented)*

---

## 4. Review & Learning Culture

**Existing**
- Failure tolerance and open sandboxing
- Project postmortems placed into a monorepo
- Diagnostic deliberation forums ("AI Doctor meeting")
- Remove older/invalidated designs for clean knowledge base
- Review and codification of tribal knowledge

**New from interviews**
- Peer-to-peer workflow sharing as a cultural norm *(n=5)*
- Context layer requires active pruning, not just growth *(n=2–4)*
- Learning loop cadence varies — per PR, daily, or weekly *(n=3 patterns)*

---

## 5. Judgment & Accountability

**Existing**
- Committer owns AI output
- Defined junction of human vs. model review
- Higher quality threshold for customer-facing AI
- Rebuilding workflows rather than layering AI on existing ones

**New from interviews**
- "Who owns the consequence if this goes wrong?" as the HITL decision rule *(n=2)*
- Human touchpoints kept for customer trust, not capability gaps *(n=3–4)*
- More autonomy internally, stricter gates externally — a designed asymmetry *(n=3)*
- One agent error can replicate hundreds of times before detection *(n=1–2)*

---

## 6. AI Economics & Value

**Existing**
- AI sits in R&D, service, and information layers within total portfolio
- Value capture shifts from T&M to outcome-based AI rate models
- Limit token burn until definitive value realization

**New from interviews**
- Model routing by task complexity as active cost management *(n=2–3)*
- Token caps by seniority, with manager-approved upgrades *(n=2)*
- Ring-fenced experimentation budget with no upfront ROI requirement *(n=2)*
- Almost no firm has token cost vs. human time comparison — unit economics missing *(n=~20)*
