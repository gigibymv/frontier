# Operating Capabilities — Transcript-Verified Slide Bullets

Use these bullets for slides when the requirement is "transcript-verified only." Excluded PPTX-only claims are listed in `analysis/operating-capabilities/insights.md`.

## 1. Org Structure & Composition

**Existing**
- Smaller teams and role compression are strongest in startup/scale-up interviews.
- AI champions and steering/governance bodies appear across startup and enterprise contexts.
- Functional boundaries are shifting, but enterprise silos remain a constraint.

**New from interviews**
- Non-engineers can produce production-grade internal tools when platform rails, permissions, and engineering gates exist *(n=1 verified, 1 partial)*.
- Senior leaders personally using AI is an adoption signal, but not a universal manager mandate *(n=2)*.
- Platform teams own deployment, permissions, and connectivity; product and ops teams consume those rails *(n=1)*.
- "Context owner" is emerging as a needed role, especially where context freshness and tribal knowledge are unresolved *(n=1 verified, 1 adjacent)*.
- Automation often shifts roles toward review, cross-selling, or higher-value work rather than simple elimination *(n=3)*.

## 2. Talent Readiness

**Existing**
- AI fluency is increasingly a hiring and role-readiness threshold.
- Role audits ask whether a role will remain viable as AI capability improves.
- Orchestration and governance capabilities are becoming more important than narrow tool use.

**New from interviews**
- Orchestration is replacing direct coding as a core engineering competency in at least one verified case *(n=1)*.
- Hiring decisions can include "will this role exist in two years?" and "could this be automated?" *(n=1)*.
- AI proficiency is described as a binary threshold rather than a graded nice-to-have *(n=2)*.
- Hallucination detection, domain knowledge replacement, and personal AI subscription as hiring signals are not transcript-verified enough for default slides.

## 3. Performance Management

**Existing**
- AI leverage is becoming a performance-management topic.
- The hard metric is not AI usage volume, but quality of orchestration and judgment.

**New from interviews**
- AI proficiency is framed as "do or die" by two interviewees, though implementation maturity varies *(n=2)*.
- No transcript-verified example shows a mature, implemented metric for orchestration quality.
- "Helping others scale" as a promotion criterion remains deck-only/unverified.

## 4. Review & Learning Culture

**Existing**
- Review, postmortems, and context codification are part of the framework.
- Tribal knowledge capture is widely important but still unsolved.

**New from interviews**
- Peer-to-peer workflow sharing is real, but mechanisms vary from informal sharing to internal marketplaces *(n=2 verified, 1 limited-context)*.
- Context maintenance needs subject-matter ownership; mature daily/weekly pruning cadences are not broadly transcript-verified *(n=1)*.
- AI Doctor meetings and fixed per-PR/daily/weekly learning cadences should not be used without re-sourcing.

## 5. Judgment & Accountability

**Existing**
- Human accountability remains attached to AI-assisted outputs.
- Customer-facing AI carries a higher trust and quality bar than internal tooling.

**New from interviews**
- "Who owns the consequence if this goes wrong?" is a practical HITL placement rule *(n=2)*.
- Human touchpoints are sometimes retained to protect customer trust, not because the model cannot complete the task *(n=1 verified, 2 adjacent)*.
- Internal tools can run with more autonomy when access is permissioned, observable, and gated by engineering-owned APIs *(n=1)*.

## 6. AI Economics & Value

**Existing**
- Token burn and AI consumption need to be tied to demonstrated value.
- Outcome/value measurement is still less mature than productivity measurement.

**New from interviews**
- Model routing by task complexity is active cost management *(n=2)*.
- Token budgeting by use-case type is verified; seniority tiers with manager-approved upgrades are not *(n=1)*.
- Ring-fenced experimentation budgets can support exploration while still shutting down unjustified cases *(n=1)*.
- Token/model cost vs. human time remains a major measurement gap across the corpus.
