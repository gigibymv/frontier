# Slide Trait Skew Rationale Table

Purpose: provide a slide-ready classification table for the exact Infrastructure, Platform, and Operating Capabilities traits. Each row says whether the trait is skewed, why it is or is not startup-skewed, and why it is or is not large-org-skewed.

Classification labels:

- `Startup-skewed`: stronger signal in startup / scale-up operating patterns, but not exclusive.
- `Large-org-skewed`: stronger signal in enterprise / large-organization operating patterns, but not exclusive.
- `Both / not specific`: meaningful on both sides; do not mark as startup- or large-org-specific.
- `Preliminary`: plausible or supported by a strong example, but low-n or too narrow for a strong slide mark.
- `Do not promote`: insufficient transcript support, likely deck-only / external research, or previously flagged as unsupported.
- `External research / not skewed`: marked with the slide's external-research diamond rather than with startup or large-org predominance.
- `Not marked skewed`: no visible startup / large-org icon on the current slide, even if the trait may be analytically skewed.

Current slide-marking rule used in this file:

- Treat only the visible startup / large-org icons as skewed selections.
- Treat blue diamonds as `External research / not skewed`.
- Treat purple fill without a startup or large-org icon as a visual highlight, not as a skewed selection.

## Use Guidance

For slide marking, the safest highlighted traits are:

- **Startup-skewed on current marked slides:** `Context lives close to work`, `Smaller team ("1 engineer builds")`, `Committer owns AI output`, `Ring-fenced experimentation budget before proven ROI`.
- **Large-org-skewed on current marked slides:** `Just-in-time context via governed store`, `Agent identity & access managed centrally`, `Formal context ownership responsibility`, `Prioritizing the upskilling of the internal workforce`, `Limit token burn until definitive value realization`.
- **External research markers on current marked slides:** `AI-native workflows (built around agents)`, `Advisor-executor-reviewer split`, `Humans-in-the-loop gates / escalation triage`, `AI champions who push adoption`, `Creation of AI steering committees`, `Classifying skills and roles by replaceability`, `Novel roles focused on orchestration and governance`, `Review and codification of "tribal" knowledge`, `Rebuilding workflows rather than AI taking over existing workflows`.

Keep `Both / not specific`, `Preliminary`, and `Do not promote` traits visually distinct from the strongest skewed claims.
