# Human Accountability Boundary

## Definition

The human accountability boundary is the point in an AI-enabled workflow where a named person or role must retain review, approval, escalation, or consequence ownership.

## Evidence Summary

- The current knowledge layer links accountability-based HITL placement, customer trust escalation, outbound approval gates, and permissioned access controls.
- The pattern is draft-only. It should not become a universal rule until workflow-specific differences are reviewed.

## Open Questions

- Which human gates are required by accountability, regulation, customer trust, or operational risk?
- Which gates can relax for internal workflows but not external/customer-facing workflows?
- How should permissioning and approval be scored without rewarding unnecessary manual review?
