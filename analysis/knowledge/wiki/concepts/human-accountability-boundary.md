# Human Accountability Boundary

## Short Definition

The human accountability boundary is where AI-enabled work requires a human owner for review, approval, escalation, or consequence ownership.

## Current Synthesis

The evidence supports a narrow principle: human control is most defensible when tied to accountability, customer trust, outbound customer communication, or high-risk access.

## Evidence-Backed Claims

- `clm-oc-accountability-based-human-control`

## Important Caveats

- This is not a universal "always keep a human in the loop" rule.
- The control should be mapped to workflow risk, customer exposure, and write-side consequences.
