# Platform

## Short Definition

Platform is the connective layer that makes AI usable in work: context assembly, tool access, orchestration, workflow execution, risk controls, learning loops, and validation surfaces.

## Current Synthesis

The current evidence base treats Platform as distinct from Operating Capabilities. Platform is about the technical and workflow substrate that enables AI-enabled work. Operating Capabilities are the organizational behaviors, roles, gates, and governance patterns that convert that substrate into business value.

The strongest current Platform-adjacent claim is still stored under Operating Capabilities because the evidence is about role and ownership boundaries: `clm-oc-platform-rails-preserve-engineering-boundaries`.

## Evidence-Backed Claims

- `clm-oc-platform-rails-preserve-engineering-boundaries` - non-engineers may produce internal tools, but deployment/runtime/access rails stay engineering-owned or permissioned.
- `clm-oc-context-ownership-emerging-role` - context ownership appears as a governance pattern adjacent to Platform context.

## Important Caveats

- Current quote-backed claims do not yet define a full Platform maturity model.
- Do not collapse Platform into Operating Capabilities. The boundary is still analytically useful.
- Some existing analysis files may use older language, but this wiki uses Platform as the live label.
