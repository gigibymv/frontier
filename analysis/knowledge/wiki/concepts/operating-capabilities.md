# Operating Capabilities

## Short Definition

Operating Capabilities are the organizational capabilities needed to convert AI into business value: roles, talent, performance management, review culture, governance, accountability, measurement, and AI economics.

## Current Synthesis

Most atomic insights currently live under Operating Capabilities because the first knowledge pass focused on behavior and governance patterns visible in the interview corpus.

The strongest current clusters are:

- accountability-based human control;
- AI fluency as a talent/performance threshold;
- workflow-owner-led automation;
- workflow-specific role redesign;
- use-case-based AI economics and budget gates.

## Important Caveats

- None of these claims are `scorecard_ready` or `slide_ready`.
- Several are draft claims with mono-source components.
- Role-change evidence should be framed as workflow-specific redirection, not broad headcount reduction.
