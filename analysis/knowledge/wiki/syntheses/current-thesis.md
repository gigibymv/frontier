# Current Thesis

## Thesis

Frontier AI operating models are emerging around a combined Platform and Operating Capabilities stack: Platform provides the rails for context, tools, orchestration, permissions, and workflow execution; Operating Capabilities determine whether humans can govern, adapt, review, reuse, and extract value from those rails.

## Evidence-Backed Claim Clusters

- Accountability-based human control: `clm-oc-accountability-based-human-control`
- AI fluency as a threshold capability: `clm-oc-ai-fluency-as-threshold`
- Workflow-owner-led automation and local sharing: `clm-oc-workflow-owners-build-local-automations`
- Engineering-owned or permissioned platform rails: `clm-oc-platform-rails-preserve-engineering-boundaries`
- Workflow-specific role redesign: `clm-oc-role-shifts-redirect-human-work`
- Token and experimentation governance: `clm-oc-token-budgeting-by-use-case`, `clm-oc-experimentation-budget-governance`

## What Is Strongest

The strongest current evidence is not that firms eliminate humans. It is that they move human control to clearer accountability boundaries, while allowing more automation inside permissioned or lower-risk work surfaces.

## What Is Still Weak

- Context ownership is promising but mono-source.
- Workflow reuse is narrower than a marketplace dynamic.
- Role redesign evidence is workflow-specific.
- Deck case studies still contain unresolved or contaminated claims.
