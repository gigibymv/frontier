# Startup vs. Enterprise Traits From Interview Transcripts

Purpose: classify the exact slide traits shown in the Infrastructure and Platform screenshots by transcript evidence. The goal is traceability: each row starts with the precise slide wording, then gives the startup / large-organization classification, evidence, and stress-test caveat.

Source standard:

- Use `interviews/transcripts/` as the source of truth.
- Classify by the organization being described, not by the folder path. Kal Gangavarapu/JPMC and Nishith Sahu/Maersk are treated as large-organization evidence even though their transcripts sit under `interviews/transcripts/startups/`.
- Treat `startup-specific` and `enterprise-specific` as high bars. In this corpus, most defensible claims are better phrased as `startup-skewed implementation style` or `enterprise-shaped governance pattern`.
- Preserve slide wording. If the slide trait is broader than the evidence, keep the slide wording in the first column and narrow the classification in the rationale.
- Do not promote known contaminated or unsupported claims unless re-sourced directly from transcripts.

## Classification Key

| Label | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Startup-skewed | Stronger transcript signal in startup / scale-up cases, but transferable to large organizations. |
| Large-org-skewed | Stronger transcript signal in enterprise, bank, logistics, manufacturing, large tech, or regulated contexts. |
| Both / not exclusive | Substantive transcript evidence appears on both sides, or the capability is too generic to classify by org type. |
| Preliminary example | A useful example, but low-n / mono-source; do not present as a general org-type trait. |
| Do not promote | Deck-only, external-research-like, contaminated, unsupported, or not directly checked enough for transcript-backed use. |

## Executive Takeaway

The exact slide traits mostly do **not** divide cleanly into startup-only versus enterprise-only. The safer pattern is:

- **Startup-skewed:** repo/workflow-local implementation style, lightweight planning gates, task-level model routing, agent-runtime craft, coding-agent sandboxes, and fixes-to-skills loops.
- **Large-org-skewed:** governed stores, central identity/access, audit trails, enterprise systems integration, formal approval gates, compliant data layers, and token / rollout controls.
- **Both:** specialized agents, scoped context, HITL gates, observability, reusable skills, AI proficiency, peer sharing, and customer-risk boundaries.

## Advisor Stress Test

1. **Transferability:** Most startup-skewed mechanics can transfer to enterprises. Once scaled, they become centralized, permissioned, logged, audited, and embedded into governance.
2. **Implementation style vs. capability:** The capability is often shared. Startup distinction is lightweight and close to work; enterprise distinction is layered and cross-functional.
3. **Evidence strength:** Strong promoted traits have multiple transcript sources or a clearly labeled low-n caveat. Mono-source rows are marked preliminary/example-only.
4. **Counterexamples:** Enterprise markdown/GitHub context weakens "context close to work is startup-only." Startup approval/HITL examples weaken "human review is enterprise-only."

Bottom line: when using the slide, keep the exact trait label visible, but present the conclusion as **startup-skewed implementation style**, **large-org-shaped governance pattern**, or **both / not exclusive** unless the transcript evidence really supports a narrower claim.
